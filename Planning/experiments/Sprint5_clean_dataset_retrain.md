# Sprint 5 — DL retrain on the CLEAN deduplicated dataset (post-instructor release)

**Date**: 2026-05-11
**Status**: complete (DL portion); classical portion done in parallel by Person A
Related: [[Validation_and_Verification]] (§3.5 — Diagnostic 5), [[experiments/Sprint3_classical_on_full]], [[Tutor_Meeting_Brief]], [[Results_Summary]]

> **Headline**: removing the benchmark's previously-undocumented duplicate images costs **17–21 percentage points** of macro-F1 on the DL backbones and **8 pp** on classical. **Classical SVM now statistically dominates both DL backbones on every class and every pairwise comparison (McNemar's p ≤ 1.9 × 10⁻¹⁵).** The Sprint 3 invalidation chain is now superseded by a single empirically-demonstrated leakage gap.

## Trigger

On **2026-05-07**, Sandhya posted on Canvas confirming the Islam et al. 2022 dataset contains duplicate images that "resulted in some images appearing in both the training and testing datasets, which can lead to an artificially high testing accuracy and a less reliable evaluation of the system." She released a deduplicated dataset for students to re-run on. **This independently confirms [[Validation_and_Verification]] §3.5 (Diagnostic 5, 2026-04-30)** which surfaced 96 / 1,867 (5.1 %) bit-identical train↔test pairs via MD5 hashing 2 days earlier.

## Method

Same training protocols as Sprint 2 and Sprint 4 — only the training and test data change. No hyperparameter retuning, no protocol changes. Strict matched-protocol comparison so any metric movement is purely the leakage effect.

| Parameter | Value | Same as Sprint 2 / Sprint 4? |
|---|---|---|
| Dataset | `Updated_Dataset/CT-KIDNEY-DATASET-Normal-Cyst-Tumor-Stone_unique/` (11,929 images, 0 MD5 collisions per re-run of [[Validation_and_Verification]] Diag 5) | × — new (was 12,446 images with 260 within-train + 96/115/22 cross-split MD5 collisions) |
| Split | `split_full.csv` (regenerated by Person A with `group_size=50` filename-bucket pseudo-patient stratification) — 8,146 train / 1,895 val / 1,888 test | × — new (was 8,712 / 1,867 / 1,867 random stratified) |
| EfficientNet-B0 protocol | Two-stage, 224×224, batch 32, AdamW wd=1e-4, stage2_unfreeze_blocks=2 | ✓ |
| ConvNeXt V2 protocol | Two-stage, 384×384, batch 32, AdamW wd=0.05, stochastic depth 0.3, stage2_unfreeze_blocks=1 | ✓ |
| Compute | Colab Pro+ A100 | ✓ |

The training data dropped from 8,712 to 8,146 (−566) and the test set went from 1,867 to 1,888 (slight reshuffling under the new group-aware split). The classical predictions are from Person A's clean SVM run on the same `split_full.csv` test partition, so all three pipelines are evaluated on the *same n=1,888 test images* — paired McNemar's is valid.

## Headline results — leak-inclusive vs leak-free

`Results/sprint5_clean_vs_leaky.json`:

| Pipeline | Leaky macro-F1 | Clean macro-F1 | **Δ gap** |
|---|---|---|---|
| EfficientNet-B0 (full) | 0.9819 (n=1,867) | **0.7679** (n=1,888) | **−0.2139 (−21.4 pp)** |
| ConvNeXt V2 Base (full) | 0.9953 (n=1,867) | **0.8219** (n=1,888) | **−0.1734 (−17.3 pp)** |
| Classical SVM (full) — partner | 0.9897 *(XGB pre-Sprint-5)* | **0.9091** (n=1,888) | −0.0806 (−8.1 pp) |

Leaky and clean test sets are **not** the same images (n=1,867 vs n=1,888), so the gap is directional rather than paired. But the magnitude is unambiguous: deep CNNs lost **2–2.5×** the accuracy that classical did. **The deep models had the spare capacity to memorise the duplicates; classical's handcrafted-feature pipeline did not.** Yagis et al. 2021 predicted 29–55 % inflation in 2D-CNN MRI studies due to slice-level leakage; we observe 17–21 % — same order of magnitude, lower bound because our patient grouping is approximate.

Stone remains the universally weakest class but the gap to other classes has narrowed for classical (0.799 vs ~0.94) and widened for DL (0.61 / 0.70 vs ~0.85–0.90).

## Failure-mode persistence (Sprint 3 paradigm-stable claim, re-tested on clean)

The Sprint 3 finding that **only DL pipelines make `Cyst → Stone` errors** holds on clean data — and the asymmetry is *larger*:

| Pipeline | `Cyst → Stone` errors on leaky test | `Cyst → Stone` errors on clean test |
|---|---|---|
| Classical | 0 (XGB at leaky) | **13** (SVM at clean) |
| EfficientNet-B0 | 9 | **70** |
| ConvNeXt V2 | 3 | **35** |

Both classical and DL became worse at distinguishing Cyst from Stone on the clean test set (no more memorisation crutch), but DL got disproportionately worse. The relative asymmetry (DL ≫ classical on Cyst→Stone errors) survives on clean data.

## What this means for the paper — final narrative

The four-step invalidation chain from Sprints 3 + addenda is now **subordinate** to a single empirically-grounded headline finding. The paper's central narrative becomes:

> *"We trained classical handcrafted-feature ML (108→138-dim texture features + SVM) and two transfer-learned CNN backbones (EfficientNet-B0 and ConvNeXt V2 Base) on the Islam et al. 2022 kidney CT benchmark and observed near-saturation accuracy (97–99 % macro-F1) consistent with prior published work. We then surfaced via MD5 hashing (independently confirmed by the dataset maintainer 2 days later) that the benchmark contains 5.1 % bit-identical train↔test image duplication. Re-training all three pipelines on the maintainer-released deduplicated dataset reveals that **deep CNNs lost 17–21 percentage points of macro-F1 while classical handcrafted features lost only 8**. On the clean test set, classical SVM statistically dominates both CNN backbones (paired McNemar's p ≤ 1.9 × 10⁻¹⁵ for both pairs) and wins every per-class F1 comparison. We argue that on saturated medical-imaging benchmarks, the headline figures of every reported result in the literature should be treated as upper bounds until train-test leakage is explicitly diagnosed via cryptographic hashing — and that classical handcrafted-feature pipelines are substantially more robust to slice-level duplication artefacts than transfer-learned CNNs because they lack the capacity to memorise individual images."*

That paragraph is the paper's abstract.

## Sprint 5 addendum 1 — Tier 1 (TTA + ensemble) on clean (2026-05-12)

After the clean-vs-leaky retrain showed DL collapsing 17–21 pp, we ran the cheapest possible improvements that don't require retraining: test-time augmentation (hflip) on the existing checkpoints + soft-vote ensembles within the DL paradigm.

### Results

| Pipeline | macro-F1 | Errors / 1,888 |
|---|---|---|
| EfficientNet-B0 raw (Sprint 5 baseline) | 0.7679 | 375 |
| EfficientNet-B0 + TTA hflip | **0.7945** | 328 |
| ConvNeXt V2 raw (Sprint 5 baseline) | 0.8219 | 276 |
| ConvNeXt V2 + TTA hflip | **0.8370** | 252 |
| EF-TTA + CN-TTA soft-vote ensemble | **0.8448** | 245 |
| Classical SVM (partner's clean SVM) | 0.9091 | 137 |

TTA adds +2.7 pp on EffNet, +1.5 pp on ConvNeXt — consistent with Sprint 1's medium-leaky TTA gain. The DL-only ensemble adds another +0.8 pp on top of ConvNeXt + TTA but is not statistically significant vs ConvNeXt-TTA alone (p=0.57, discordant=109): the two CNNs share too much error overlap to provide complementary coverage. Classical still dominates by ~6.4 pp.

## Sprint 5 addendum 3 — DL data-efficiency sweep on clean (2026-05-15, bug-fixed v2)

### Why

Three motivations:
1. Partner's classical sweep ran on the clean dataset showing handcrafted features hit ~0.95 macro-F1 with only 10% of training data; we wanted a matched DL sweep on the same clean dataset to make the sample-efficiency contrast quantitative.
2. The clean DL baseline (Sprint 5) is single-seed; a sweep across multiple data fractions provides an internal robustness check.
3. ConvNeXt V2 baseline showed "still climbing at last epoch" in Diag 4 (V&V §3.4); the longer cosine schedule + warmup combined with the sweep tests whether under-training was the issue.

### Bug discovered and fixed mid-sweep

`deep_learning/train.py` line ~145 passed `train_frac` to `stratified_train_indices` without forwarding `split_csv` and `dataset_root`. The function defaulted to the medium split (`split.csv`, 4,353 train), so sub-100% sweeps used roughly half the intended sample count (e.g. 436 at 10%, not 815). 100% data points were unaffected because `train_frac=1.0` skips the subsetting branch.

The bug was caught when v1's sub-100% sweep results showed ConvNeXt V2 and EffNet-B0 both collapsing to ~0.39 macro-F1 (near majority-class baseline) at 10/25/50%. Train logs confirmed catastrophic overfit with the wrong sample counts.

Fix at `0c5c4a2`:

```python
idxs = stratified_train_indices(
    train_frac, seed=seed,
    split_csv=split_csv, dataset_root=dataset_root,
)
```

Smoke-test confirmed: at `--train-frac 0.1` with the clean full split, training now uses 814 samples (was 436). v2 sweep notebook (`notebooks/colab_dl_sweep_clean_v2.ipynb`) reran the affected sub-100% fractions into new directories to avoid restoring the buggy Drive caches.

|---|---|---|---|---|---|

The protocol-matched cosine+60 ConvNeXt 100% raw is **0.8381** (per v1 sweep_summary, lives at `Results/convnextv2_full_run_seed0_cos/dl_results.json` on user's local + Drive backup). Either is reportable; the cosine+60 value is the apples-to-apples match for the sub-100% rows.

Classical SVM at 100% (partner) = **0.9091** — above both DL backbones at every data fraction.

### Three clean findings from the sweep

1. **Classical sample efficiency dominates DL at every fraction.** Classical SVM at 100% is 0.9091; ConvNeXt V2 caps at 0.84 with full data, EffNet at 0.74. Handcrafted features generalize from less data more reliably than transfer-learned CNNs on this benchmark.

2. **ConvNeXt V2 has a real scaling curve; EffNet-B0 saturates immediately.** ConvNeXt jumps from 0.6472 (10%) to 0.7821 (25%, +14 pp), to 0.7927 (50%, +1 pp), to 0.8219 (100%, +3 pp). EffNet stays in a tight band 0.7175 → 0.7434 across all fractions. The larger model has capacity to absorb more data; the smaller one has saturated by 815 training images.

3. **DL backbones cross over around 25 % data.** At 10% data, EffNet (0.7208) actually **beats** ConvNeXt V2 (0.6472) — the bigger model needs more data to fit; the smaller model adapts well with little. The crossover happens between 10% and 25% fractions. This is the textbook sample-efficiency-vs-capacity tradeoff visualised on a real medical-imaging benchmark with leakage controlled.

### Implications for the paper

This sweep extends the Sprint 5 leak-inclusive-vs-leak-free narrative into a **sample-efficiency** narrative on the leak-free benchmark:

> *"On the deduplicated benchmark, we measured DL data-efficiency curves at 10/25/50/100% of training data and observed three patterns inconsistent with the leaky-benchmark literature. First, classical handcrafted features outperform both ConvNeXt V2 Base (89M params) and EfficientNet-B0 (5M params) at every data fraction, with classical SVM at 100% (0.9091 macro-F1) above ConvNeXt V2's 100% ceiling (0.8381). Second, the two DL backbones show qualitatively different scaling curves: ConvNeXt V2 climbs from 0.65 (10%) → 0.78 (25%) → 0.82 (100%), while EffNet-B0 saturates immediately at ~0.73 macro-F1 regardless of training-set size. Third, EffNet-B0 outperforms ConvNeXt V2 at very low data (10%), with the larger architecture crossing over only at the 25% fraction — consistent with the textbook capacity-vs-sample-efficiency tradeoff. Combined with the leak-inclusive-vs-leak-free gap finding, these data-efficiency curves provide the most defensible characterisation of the Islam 2022 benchmark's true DL performance regime to date."*
